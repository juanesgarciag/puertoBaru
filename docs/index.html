about us

Puerto Baru (Desarrollo Multimodal Baru, SRL) is a consortium of US led investors and Panamanian industry experts that include the Previous administrator of the Panama Canal, CEO of one of the largest marine construction companies in Panama, previous KPMG executive and international infrastructure financier.

The synergy of the Puerto Baru team combined with their industry expertise, common thread of thinking outside of the box and alignment of putting people, planet and profits (in that order) enables us to not just develop a port, but develop something that will enrich the lives of the people of Chiriquí for numerous generations.

our history

the invisible billionaire

Daniel Ludwig, also known as the Invisible Billionaire, is the master mind with originally identifying and starting to develop the port. He is the visionary behind Puerto Baru back in the 1960’s as well as dozens of other projects and businesses in numerous countries around the world. Mr. Ludwig planted over 10,000 acres of citrus trees in the Chiriquí Province.

He dredged the river mouth to the port location. His goal was to bring in tankers, load them up with oranges and process the oranges into concentrate on his tankers and deliver them to markets around the world. Unfortunately, his project was nationalized in the late 80’s and his plan never came to fruition. The site was eventually auctioned off by the Panamanian government and picked up by a private landowner where it sat dormant.

After a half dozen years of studies, reports and negotiations, we were able to take control of the property and continue with Daniel Ludwig’s vision of creating a feeder port to service the Chiriquí Province and beyond.

the project

we connect possibilities

Puerto Barú Multimodal will export agricultural goods and import containers, fuel, sand, fertilizer, bulk goods, farm equipment and grain. As well as provide a place for mini cruises to dock in order to visit the beautiful towns in and around Chiriqui such as Boquete.

The Panamanian Government has been researching possible feeder port locations in Northern (Pacific Side) Panama and recently spent millions of dollars to explore and investigate possible feeder port locations in the Chiriqui Province. The combination of massive protected mangroves, the inability to find protected water areas as well as disconnect between the private and public sector, has made finding any potential feeder port in the area extremely difficult.

the team

Puerto Barú is spearheaded
by Alberto Alemán Zubieta.

Alberto Alemán Zubieta is the former CEO of the Panama Canal Authority, the autonomous agency that manages the Panama Canal, the leading interoceanic waterway that serves world maritime commerce.

Alberto Alemán Zubieta began his professional career in the construction field. For many years, he served as CEO of one of the largest construction companies in the Republic of Panama. From 1996 to 1999, he served as Administrator of the former Panama Canal Commission, the United States Government federal agency which was responsible, until December 31, 1999, for the administration and operation of the Panama Canal.

In 1998, two years into his appointment as head of the Panama Canal Commission, he was appointed Administrator of the Panama Canal Authority, the Panamanian entity tasked with the administration of the waterway upon its transfer to the Republic of Panama, on December 31st, 1999. During the following two years, he served simultaneously as Administrator for both the Panama Canal Commission and the Panama Canal Authority.
